Why we built BAM

Post #1 — by the BAM team

Most messaging on Ethereum today is framed as transactions. That's expensive (gas paid per sender, per message), it tightly couples authorship to a contract account, and it makes large multi-sender event streams (timelines, comment threads, feeds) impractical.

BAM — Blob Authenticated Messaging — sidesteps the transaction frame. A single sender signs an EIP-712-typed claim over a per-sender monotonic nonce; an aggregator batches many senders into a blob and posts one transaction. The on-chain contract verifies the batch, indexes a per-tag content stream, and emits the data so any reader can reconstruct the messages.

The trust model is small enough to write down on an index card: each individual signature must be valid; the aggregator can pick which messages to include but can't forge them. The remainder is "blockchain as a notarised log of who said what".
